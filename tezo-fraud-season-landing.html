Fair questions

What the skeptics ask us first.

Do you replace our validation team?

No, and we would decline the engagement if that were the ask. Your validators own every opinion and every sign-off. What we add is the engineering they were never staffed for: the drift monitors, adversarial suites, and load harnesses that turn their challenge from an annual exercise into a weekly instrument. Our clients’ validators don’t get replaced; they get instruments.

You build AI systems. How is your testing independent?

Structurally. The assurance team is separate from model development — Tezo’s own included — with no stake in any model being approved. That separation is SR 11-7’s definition of effective challenge, and it is the first thing we put in the SOW. We test what others build; we never mark our own homework.

Is there enough time before this peak season?

If you start now, yes. Ninety days is enough to reach instrumented readiness on the models you already have: visibility in the first month, stress testing in the second, rehearsal and evidence in the third. What starting later deletes first is adversarial depth — the countdown at the top of this page is not a design flourish.

What do you need from us to start?

Less than you would expect: read access to model inventories and monitoring data, a sandbox or shadow path for load and adversarial work, and one owner on your side. No production write access, and your data does not leave your environment. The readout itself needs nothing but thirty minutes.

We run vendor fraud models. Can you test those?

That is often the most valuable place to start. Regulators hold you to the same standard for purchased models, and vendor self-attestation is not independent evidence. We test their model on your data under your peak conditions — and give your due-diligence file something no vendor can produce about themselves.
